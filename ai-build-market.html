Frequently Asked Questions

What is AI Build Market?

AI Build Market is an online marketplace where people who build AI tools, AI-powered businesses, prompt packs, product ideas, and games can list them for sale directly to buyers. The platform takes a transaction fee only when a sale completes.

Where can I sell an AI tool I built?

AI Build Market lets you list an AI tool for free and puts it in front of buyers actively browsing by category, without needing to run your own ads. A transaction fee applies only when the tool sells.

Can I sell an AI-built business on AI Build Market?

Yes. AI Build Market has a dedicated Businesses category for AI-built or AI-automated businesses, including sites, automation stacks, and licensable systems. Higher-value business listings go through identity verification on both sides before the sale completes.

How much does AI Build Market charge to sell something?

Listing is free. AI Build Market charges a transaction fee only on completed sales, so sellers pay nothing upfront to list an AI tool, business, prompt pack, idea, or game.

Is AI Build Market responsible for what's sold on the platform?

No. AI Build Market is a venue connecting independent buyers and sellers. Every sale is a direct agreement between buyer and seller, and all listings are sold as-is unless a seller states an additional guarantee in writing on the listing.

Terms Summary — Legal Framework Built Into AI Build Market

Not legal advice. This is a structural outline of the clauses a marketplace like this typically needs — have an actual attorney draft the binding ToS/Seller Agreement before launch, especially the arbitration and tax sections, which are jurisdiction-specific.

1. MARKETPLACE FACILITATOR, NOT SELLER

AI Build Market is defined as a venue connecting independent buyers and sellers. Title to any tool, code, business entity, prompt, or game transfers directly from seller to buyer. AI Build Market never takes ownership of listed goods and disclaims any role as manufacturer, developer, or warrantor of what's sold.

2. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY / NO POST-SALE RESPONSIBILITY

Once a transaction completes, AI Build Market's liability ends. No responsibility for bugs, downtime, revenue claims, business viability, IP validity, or any consequential/incidental damages. Liability capped at fees actually collected on that transaction (standard marketplace cap — protects against open-ended exposure).

3. AS-IS / NO WARRANTY

All goods sold "as-is." Any performance claims, revenue numbers, or guarantees come from the seller, not AI Build Market, and are the seller's sole representation and liability.

4. IP OWNERSHIP & AI-GENERATED CONTENT DISCLOSURE

Sellers must warrant they have the right to sell what they're listing. Sellers must disclose that a listing was AI-generated or AI-assisted — this matters because AI-generated output has unsettled copyright status in the U.S. (Copyright Office has denied protection for purely AI-generated works). Buyers are told plainly: they may be buying something with limited or no copyright protection, and that's on them to evaluate before buying.

5. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Mandatory good-faith negotiation window, then binding individual arbitration (AAA or similar), class action waiver. This is the single biggest liability shield for a marketplace at scale — one lawsuit becoming a class action is the scenario that kills platforms like this.

6. ESCROW / PAYMENT HANDLING

Funds held until delivery is confirmed (access granted, repo transferred, business docs handed off), then released to seller minus fee. This should run through Paid! since it's already your Stripe automation layer — Stripe Connect (destination charges) is the standard rail for marketplace split payments and 1099-K reporting per seller.

7. MARKETPLACE FACILITATOR TAX LAWS

Most U.S. states now legally require the marketplace (not the seller) to collect and remit sales tax above certain thresholds. This needs a tax engine (Stripe Tax or similar) wired in before you're doing real volume — this is one regulators actually enforce.

8. PROHIBITED LISTINGS

No stolen code/IP, no malware or exploit tools, no regulated financial products (unlicensed lending, securities, trading signals sold as guaranteed returns), no adult content, no anything requiring a license the seller doesn't hold (e.g., selling a "business" that's actually an unlicensed financial services operation).

9. KYC ON HIGH-VALUE LISTINGS

Anything sold as a "business" (LLC, revenue-generating asset, domain portfolio, etc.) above a set dollar threshold should require identity verification on both sides — this is standard for M&A-adjacent marketplaces and reduces fraud and chargeback risk substantially.
